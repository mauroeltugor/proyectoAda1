Soccer

meaning

Soccer, also known as football in many countries outside of the United States,
is a sport that is played by two teams of 11 players each, with the objective of scoring goals
by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal. The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at either end,
and the team that scores the most goals by the end of the game is the winner.

rules

  1. Two teams of 11 players each compete to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team's goal.
  2. The game is played on a rectangular field with a goal at either end.
  3. Players are not allowed to touch the ball with their hands or arms, except for the goalkeeper within their own penalty area.
  4. Fouls can result in free kicks or penalties, and players can be cautioned or sent off with a red card for serious offenses.
  5. Offside is called if a player is closer to the opponent's goal than the ball and the second-last defender when a pass is made to them.
  6. The team with the most goals at the end of the game wins, with ties being allowed in some competitions and requiring extra time or
    penalty shootouts in others.

Basketball

meaning

Basketball is a team sport played on a rectangular court, where two teams of five players each try to score points by shooting a ball through a hoop
mounted 10 feet high on a backboard at each end of the court. The game involves passing, dribbling, and shooting the ball, and players must constantly
move and make split-second decisions to outmaneuver their opponents. The team with the most points at the end of the game, which usually lasts for four quarters of 12 minutes each,
wins. Basketball is not just a sport, but also a cultural phenomenon that has a global following, with millions of fans and players around the world.

rules

  1. Dribbling: Players are not allowed to carry the ball while moving. They must bounce the ball off the ground with one hand while walking or running to advance it up the court.
  2. Passing: Players may pass the ball to a teammate using their hands. The ball can be passed in any direction, but the pass must not be thrown too hard or too high.
  3. Shooting: Players score points by shooting the ball into the opponent's basket. A successful shot from within the three-point line
    is worth two points, while a shot from beyond the three-point line is worth three points.
  4. Fouls: Players must not physically interfere with other players. Contact that is deemed excessive, rough, or unsportsmanlike is called a foul.
    Players can be penalized with a loss of possession or free throws awarded to the other team.
  5. Time Limits: A regulation basketball game consists of four quarters, each lasting 12 minutes. If the game is tied after regulation, additional time is added until one team wins.
  6. Substitutions: Coaches are allowed to substitute players in and out of the game during timeouts, free throws, and dead ball situations.
    However, the same player cannot re-enter the game after being substituted out.

Ping-Pong

meaning

Ping Pong is a term commonly used to refer to the sport of table tennis. It is a game played with small paddles and a lightweight ball on a table divided
by a net. The objective of the game is to hit the ball back and forth over the net, with the goal of making the ball land on the opponent's side of the table,
and thus score a point. The name "ping pong" was derived from the sound made by the ball when it is hit back and forth across the table. Ping pong is a popular
sport played both recreationally and competitively all over the world, and is even an Olympic sport.

rules

  1. Service: A player must hit the ball from behind the end of the table, with the ball resting on an open palm, and the ball must be tossed upwards at least 6 inches before being hit.
  2. Return: The ball must be returned over the net and hit the opponent's side of the table. The ball cannot touch the net or edges of the table during the rally.
  3. Scoring: Points are awarded to the player who wins a rally. A game is typically played to 11 points, and the winner must be ahead by at least two points.
  4. Change of Service: After every two points, the player who served last must give the ball to the opponent to serve.
  5. Let: A let is called if the ball hits the net on a serve, and the ball is re-served without penalty.
  6. Doubles: In doubles play, teammates take turns serving the ball and players must alternate hitting the ball.
    The ball must also bounce on each side of the table at least once during a rally.
